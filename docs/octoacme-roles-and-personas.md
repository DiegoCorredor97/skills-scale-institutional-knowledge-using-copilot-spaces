# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Product Owner

### Role Summary
The Product Owner represents stakeholder and customer interests, maintains the product vision, and ensures the team is building features that deliver maximum value. They work closely with Product Managers and Project Managers to clarify priorities and scope.

### Responsibilities
- Define and communicate the product vision and strategy
- Clarify feature requirements and acceptance criteria
- Prioritize the backlog based on business value and customer needs
- Collaborate with stakeholders to identify and address risks
- Validate completed work against success criteria
- Bridge communication between technical and business teams

### Goals
- Ensure delivered features align with customer needs
- Maximize ROI on development efforts
- Maintain clear, shared understanding of product direction

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- Works with **Product Managers** to refine strategy and metrics
- Partners with **Project Managers** on scope and timeline trade-offs
- Collaborates with **Developers** on requirement clarification and acceptance testing
- Coordinates with **Business Stakeholders** and **Executive Sponsors** on strategic direction

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and backlog refinement sessions
- Requirements documentation and user stories
- Stakeholder demos and feedback sessions
- Feature specification documents

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
The QA Lead ensures that deliverables meet quality standards and organizational expectations. They organize testing activities, identify defects, and work with developers to maintain high-quality outputs throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain test plans and test cases
- Organize and coordinate testing activities across the team
- Identify, document, and track defects
- Ensure quality gates are met before release
- Provide feedback on code quality and usability
- Advise on automation opportunities and testing best practices
- Report quality metrics and trends to leadership

### Goals
- Deliver defect-free or minimal-defect releases
- Reduce post-release issues and customer impact
- Establish confidence in product quality
- Continuously improve testing efficiency

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- Partners with **Developers** on defect resolution and test coverage
- Works with **Project Managers** on quality timelines and resource allocation
- Supports **Product Owners** in acceptance testing
- Communicates quality status to **Executive Sponsors** and stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Test status reports and quality dashboards
- Defect tracking and escalation meetings
- Code review participation for testability
- Release readiness assessments

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
The Change Manager leads organizational adoption and transition efforts associated with new processes, tools, or significant feature releases. They ensure smooth adoption by managing communication, training, and stakeholder feedback.

### Responsibilities
- Develop change management plans for major initiatives
- Create communication materials and training resources
- Identify and address resistance to change
- Coordinate training sessions and rollout activities
- Gather feedback during and after implementation
- Document lessons learned and best practices
- Support teams through transition periods

### Goals
- Minimize disruption during transitions
- Maximize user adoption and satisfaction
- Capture feedback for continuous improvement
- Build organizational capability for future changes

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- Partners with **Project Managers** on rollout planning and timelines
- Works with **Developers** to understand technical changes for training
- Collaborates with **Product Owners** on feature adoption strategy
- Reports to **Executive Sponsors** on adoption metrics and risks

### Typical Communication
- Change impact assessments and communication plans
- Training schedules and materials
- Feedback surveys and adoption metrics
- Stakeholder town halls and Q&A sessions

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions. They gather requirements, analyze processes, and identify improvement opportunities. They work across business and technical teams to ensure solutions solve real problems.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct business process analysis and identify improvement opportunities
- Gather and document requirements from stakeholders
- Create process diagrams, flowcharts, and process documentation
- Analyze data and metrics to support decision-making
- Validate that solutions meet business objectives
- Identify and communicate risks and dependencies
- Support system configuration and implementation

### Goals
- Ensure solutions address real business problems
- Improve operational efficiency through analysis
- Bridge communication between business and IT teams
- Reduce rework through thorough requirements gathering

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- Works with **Product Managers** and **Product Owners** on requirement prioritization
- Collaborates with **Developers** on implementation feasibility
- Supports **Project Managers** with detailed scope definition
- Advises **Change Managers** on process impact and adoption strategy

### Typical Communication
- Requirements specifications and process documentation
- Analysis reports and recommendations
- Stakeholder interviews and workshops
- Process flowcharts and business case analyses

---

## Executive Sponsor

### Role Summary
The Executive Sponsor provides strategic leadership, removes organizational roadblocks, and advocates for the project at the senior leadership level. They ensure alignment with organizational strategy and secure necessary resources and support.

### Responsibilities
- Establish strategic direction and business case for the project
- Remove organizational barriers and escalated roadblocks
- Secure and allocate necessary resources and budget
- Communicate project importance to senior stakeholders
- Make trade-off decisions between competing priorities
- Review and approve major milestones and deliverables
- Champion adoption and organizational change

### Goals
- Ensure project aligns with organizational strategy
- Maintain executive support and resource commitment
- Enable team success through effective sponsorship
- Maximize business value and return on investment

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- Works with **Project Managers** on governance, escalations, and resource decisions
- Partners with **Product Owners** on strategic direction and priority alignment
- Supports **Change Managers** in building organizational support for adoption
- Communicates with **Business Analysts** on business case and ROI

### Typical Communication
- Executive steering committee meetings
- High-level status reports and business metrics
- Escalation resolution and resource decisions
- Strategic alignment and organizational announcements
